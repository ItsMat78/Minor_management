Core Objective
11/03/26 <number> International Institute of Information Technology, Naya Raipur
A centralized web application built to automate group formation, project
proposals, evaluations, and administrative oversight at IIIT-Naya Raipur.
Currently, managing minor projects across different branches (CSE, ECE, DSAI)
involves a lot of manual coordination—forming groups, assigning faculty
guides, tracking proposals, and conducting evaluations.
Our portal solves this by providing a unified, role-based platform. Whether you
are a student looking to form a cross-branch team, a faculty member managing
multiple project groups, or an admin overseeing deadlines and limits, our
application digitalizes and automates the entire workflow from semester start
to final grading.

Student Experience & Group Formation
11/03/26 <number> International Institute of Information Technology, Naya Raipur
The Student Journey (Flowchart/Steps):
Onboarding: Secure login via Institute Email (auto-detects
branch/details).
Dashboard: Personalized view of deadlines and current
status.
Group Formation:
Search for batchmates by name, roll number, or branch.
Cross-branch teams are fully supported (up to 3
members).
Surplus Pool: Visibility of students without groups to ensure
everyone finds a team.
Key Features:
Reversible groups (ability to dissolve a group before a
proposal is approved).

Project Proposal & Mentor Allocation
11/03/26 <number> International Institute of Information Technology, Naya Raipur
The Proposal Process (Step-by-Step):
Drafting: Group inputs Title, Objectives, Tags, & optional Attachments
(e.g., PDFs).
Faculty Selection: Browse available faculty showing their current load
(e.g., "5/7 groups").
Submission: Instant notification sent to the selected faculty member.
Faculty Dashboard & Review:
Review: Faculty views proposal details and group member profiles.
Action Flow:
✅ Approve (1-click approval).
❌ Reject (Mandatory feedback required).
Capacity Controls:
Strict limits enforced (Max 21 students or 7 groups per faculty).
